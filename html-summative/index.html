Computer Part Picking and Assembly

Choosing Your Parts:

Central Processing Unit or CPU

The CPU is the brain of the computer and is a very important part of your PC. So it should be one of the most exepsnive parts of your build. Depending on what you will be using your computer for you should should spend more or less on it. If you plan on doing CPU intensive tasks you should get a more powerful CPU. Although you have to make sure it is compatible with the rest of your parts.

Motherboard

The Motherboard is also a very important part of your computer. It holds your CPU, your RAM and it also has your expansion slots. You have to make sure it has enough expansion slots, you have to check the NIC's connection type (wired or wireless), you have to make sure it supports the RAM speed and amount you want and finally it must have the same socket type as your CPU.

Random Access Memory or RAM

The RAM is used constantly and it is very important to have enough RAM for what you plan on using your computer for. Doing things like opening multiple tabs and using multiple programs at once can require plenty of RAM. Especially gaming. For basic usage anything from 4 to 8 gigabytes should do. Although 8 gigabytes is probably on the lower end of gaming computers. If you plan on running heavy games you will want at least 16 gigabytes.

Storage

There are multiple types of storage devices. There are Hard Drives and Solid State Drives as well as a hybrid between the two. Hard Drives are cheaper and give you more storage, but they have worse performance than Solid State Drives. Solid State Drives have better performance, although they are considerably more expensive and have less storage per dollar. Many people buy one of each type of drive and store certain things they want to load faster, on their SSD. They also have an HDD so they have a large amount of storage if they need it.

Graphics Processing Unit

The Graphics Processor can be inside of your CPU or in the form of a dedicated graphics card. If you plan on gaming it is better to have a dedicated GPU. If you don't plan on doing to many graphic heavy tasks a CPU with a GPU inside will do fine.

Case and Fans

Computers run hot, so a good case, case fans and a CPU fan are important. First of all, your case must be big enough to fit your motherbaord. So if you have a large ATX form factor motherboard, you will have at least a mid size tower. In larger towers you will also be able to fit more fans. Secondly, case fans are important to keep high airflow. If you have a large powerful dedicated graphics card case fans can help keep it cool. Finally a good CPU fan is important. The CPU is one of the hottest things in your computer.

Power Supply

The power supply provides power to every component in your computer. If you have a powerful CPU and dedicated GPU you will need a more powerful power supply. There are power supply wattage calculators online. Just enter your parts and it will tell you how many watts you will need. Make sure to check for modularity. Fully modular means you can remove and add cables as you please. Non-modular means all of the cables are permanently attached. Finally there is Semi-modular which means that some of the cables can be removed and attached.
